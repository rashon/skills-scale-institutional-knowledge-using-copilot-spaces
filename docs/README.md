## OctoAcme Project Management Process Summary

OctoAcme’s project management practices provide a robust framework for consistent, high-quality project delivery guided by clarity, communication, and continuous improvement. The process is structured around five key phases:

1. **Initiation:** Validate business need, clarify outcomes, and align stakeholders using a project one-pager, risk list, and communication plan.
2. **Planning:** Break work down with prioritized backlog, clear responsibilities, dependencies, and the Definition of Done.
3. **Execution & Tracking:** Use manageable pull requests, CI/CD quality gates, regular standups, and risk reviews to ensure focus, quality, and transparency.
4. **Release & Deployment:** Rely on documented checklists, automated tests, and incident playbooks to deliver with confidence and recover quickly from issues.
5. **Continuous Improvement:** Conduct retrospectives after major milestones and incidents, turning learnings into action items tracked alongside project work.

### Roles & Communication
- **Project Manager:** Coordinates delivery, reporting, and risk management.
- **Product Manager:** Defines outcomes and priorities, measures value.
- **Developers:** Implement, contribute to design/review, maintain code/test quality.
- **QA:** Ensure changes meet all requirements before ship.
- **Stakeholders:** Informed and engaged via regular updates, with clear escalation paths for blockers.

Regular communication cadences (standups, syncs, demos, stakeholder updates) and a living risk register ensure issues are visible and addressed early. All process improvements—and proposed documentation changes—use tracked issues and structured templates to standardize and improve knowledge sharing.

See each guide for detailed workflows, templates, and escalation guidance. These living documents evolve as the team learns and grows. For details, start with the “Project Management Overview” or your role-specific persona.
